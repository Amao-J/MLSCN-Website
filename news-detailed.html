AMLSN EXCO Visits MLSCN Normalize Relations

15 February 2023 | 5 mins

Corporate Affairs Department

Featured AMLSN News

The Registrar/CEO, Dr. Tosan Erhabor has announced the appointment of Ms Ndidiamaka Okafor, a Cross River State-based Medical Laboratory Scientist, as MLSCN Ambassador on Malaria Microscopy. This was contained in the Registrar's remarks in Abuja, on Monday, July 31, 2023, while receiving Okafor from Kenya where MLSCN had sponsored her for further exposure on malaria microscopy. He also reiterated MLSCN's commitment towards the eradication of malaria in the country.

Erhabor justified the beneficiary's selection as purely based on merit, adding that she had no relationship with the Council's management but benefitted from the Council's commitment towards the eradication of malaria in the country. "Driven by such a commitment, the management had nominated to sponsor to Nairobi, Kenya, any participant that achieved Level One at the National Competence Assessment on Malaria Microscopy (NCAMM). Okafor emerged the sole winner of the prize, and was supposed to travel to Kenya last year, but the trip did not take place. As an effort to sponsor her yielded the desired results and was recognized by the operation of the Federal Ministry of Health," he said. Moreover, he enquired about the challenges the MLSCN Ambassador observed and if the acquired skills could be employed in the country to build adequate microscopy for combating the scourge of malaria.

Furthermore, the Registrar assured the Malaria Microscopy Ambassador of his Council's desire to continue to work with her and urged her to henceforth see herself as representing MLSCN at any official function on malaria microscopy. He praised her for being steadfast throughout the rigorous selection process.

Okafor expressed gratitude to the Council for the support given to her, noting that the trip was totally unexpected and placed more demand on her, more so because it was her first international exposure. "For me, the trip to Kenya was an eye-opener to the way malaria microscopy should be done, as I was able to acquire more skills, which I believe the exposure I gained will surely serve me well in malaria microscopy. Working with MLSCN to help eradicate malaria is a lifetime opportunity, which I would never take for granted," she said.
